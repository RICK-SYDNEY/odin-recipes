DELICIOUS BANKU

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn and cassava dough. It is typically served with soup, stew, or grilled fish. This recipe will guide you through the process of making authentic banku at home.

How to Make Banku

Making banku requires some patience due to the fermentation process, but the end result is a delicious and satisfying dish. Below are the basic steps to prepare banku:

Banku Ingredients

Ingredients to have before we start are as follows:

  • Fermented corn dough (Kenkey dough)
  • Cassava dough
  • Water
  • Salt (optional)

The key to great banku lies in the quality of the fermented corn and cassava dough. You can find these ingredients at African grocery stores or make them yourself if you have the time.

STEPS

  1. Mix the fermented corn dough and cassava dough in a large pot.
  2. Add water gradually while stirring to avoid lumps.
  3. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring continuously.
  4. Once it thickens, reduce the heat and cover the pot.
  5. Let it cook for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Serve hot with your choice of soup or stew.
